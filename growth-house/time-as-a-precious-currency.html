Time as a Precious Currency

11 May 2026

Time has quietly become one of our most precious currencies. The busier life gets, the more of it we seem to need — and the harder it becomes to find. Yet it remains the one thing we all share equally: 24 hours. 1,440 minutes. Every day.

The question behind the question

We all have things we want to do — but we "can't find the time." So let me ask a few questions worth sitting with:

  • · What is valuable to you?
  • · What makes you happy?
  • · What do you want to focus on?
  • · What do you prioritize?
  • · What do you invest in?

And most importantly: what could you remove or scale down to make room for what you need and want?

What people don't see

People sometimes ask me: "How do you manage to do X?" The answer is prioritizing. People see the results of my choices — not what I've said no to, removed, or scaled down.

Over the years, I've learned that saying no is often what protects the things I truly care about.

A small spring invitation

It's spring — so let me propose a small personal spring cleaning. Why not take a short walk? Sweden is about 70% forest, so chances are there's a trail or a park close by. Invest 10–20 minutes. It's worth it.

Ask yourself:

  • · What do you want to prioritize next week?
  • · What do you want to say yes to?
  • · And if you say yes — what might you need to say no to?

Repeat weekly. Build the habit of choosing your "yes."

Time is precious. Choose wisely — because the choices you make today shape your tomorrow.
