Happy Folklore Day — A Simple Idea with Big Impact

21 April 2026

If you've ever prepared for a customer meeting or an internal presentation, you know the first challenge is rarely the content. It's everything around it — the detective work of figuring out what's true, what's outdated, and what still lives in someone's head rather than in a system.

We've all been there:

  • ·The data is there, but the key information still lives in someone's head
  • ·The templates exist, but they're outdated — and you're left choosing between chasing someone or fixing them yourself
  • ·Someone is out of office, and no one knows when they'll be back

And it's not just a feeling. McKinsey highlights that 70% of all Customer Success expertise is undocumented — pure folklore. They call this missing piece folklore data, and Anthropic describes it as the gap between data and relevance.

Why does this stay status quo?

Because folklore has no owner. It exists in every organisation, regardless of size or geography. And the uncomfortable truth is that everyone is responsible for documenting, updating and sharing information internally — which is exactly why it gets postponed.

I'll do it tomorrow. Next week. Next quarter.
Recognise the collective "Any year now"?

We all do this every day — me too. And the folklore adds up.

Let's make a yearly Folklore Day

We used to have a CRM Cleaning Day once a year: a few hours of updating the CRM, with snacks and music, where everyone contributed something small. The result was huge — better quality, fewer gaps and smoother handovers. Everyone won.

A Folklore Day works the same way.

Three hours.
Everyone adds 1–5 pieces of missing context.
Small efforts → big impact.

It's about taking responsibility as a team and as an organisation — improving data quality and surfacing the knowledge that used to live in people's heads. When many people make small adjustments, the collective effect becomes significant.

How would a Folklore Day look in your organisation?

Happy Folklore Day. 🎉
