Brain Health Is Good Business

1 July 2026

Have you heard of the equation "brain health + brain skills = brain capital"?

I came across it last week during McKinsey's 30‑minute webinar Brain health: Helping organizations and societies thrive in the age of AI.

  • Brain health: Optimal brain functioning across the lifespan.
  • Brain skills: Cognitive, interpersonal, self‑leadership and technological literacy abilities.
  • Brain capital: A driver of long‑term economic and social value — the combination of the two, and a strategic economic asset for organizations.

The last part is important, since brain health is often framed as an individual responsibility, but McKinsey was clear: companies have a role, and an economic incentive, to protect and strengthen brain capital.

AI and technology are incredible amplifiers. They help us think, create and produce more. But they also tend to generate more workload than anticipated. Suddenly you're processing more information, handling more ideas, and working with more tasks in parallel.

Yes, AI amplifies you, but there is still only one you and you have one brain.

Balancing these possibilities with sustainable brain health isn't just good for people, it's good business. Scale that across an entire organization, and the impact becomes significant.

With summer holidays approaching, it's a good moment to pause, reflect and maybe take some time off. A chance to think about sustainability, how to work smarter, and how to protect brain health in the long run.

A true win‑win for both people and organizations.
