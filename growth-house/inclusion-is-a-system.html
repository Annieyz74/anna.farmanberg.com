Inclusion Is a System, Not Just a Value

9 July 2026

When was the last time you practiced your sport together with someone aged 80+? We recently brought an 81-year-old gentleman with us for a round. He rode a golf cart, which he regretted, but he could still enjoy his favourite sport.

Golf is a wonderfully inclusive sport where everyone, regardless of age or skill level, can play together, because the handicap system and different hole lengths make it possible. Visit any golf course in Sweden and the chances are high that you'll see children and 70+ year olds playing side by side. And Pay & play clubs make the sport even more accessible.

Low intensity exercise, fresh air, social yet individual, where strategy and mindset mean everything. More experienced players model good pace of play and share etiquette and rules when needed. (Unsolicited tips are, however, taboo.)

I've been thinking about what would happen if companies took a bit of inspiration from golf's mindset and culture. Inclusion, a mix of people, backgrounds and skill levels working together and creating a pleasant atmosphere.

Golf reminds us that inclusion isn't just a value, it's a system and those can be built.

I know that many companies do this or try to already. We often talk about inclusion, but I believe that we rarely build structures that actually make it possible. Golf's handicap system is really just one thing: a structure that allows people of different ages and abilities to play the same game.

I believe working life would benefit from more structures like that.
