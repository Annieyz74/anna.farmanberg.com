How I Work in Practice

When I needed a personal website, I approached it as a design challenge. I started with the idea of a clean, "less is more" approach. I wanted to learn how to build it from scratch using HTML, so I began with a simple MVP. I tested different layouts and iterated until the structure felt clean and purposeful. I focused on what mattered most: usability, personal brand, and a professional tone. And I removed anything that did not add value.

I also reached out to a few people for feedback, and the result is a personal, clean, functional site that reflects how I think and work. The experience reinforced my belief in starting small, learning fast, improving continuously, and asking for constructive feedback. I apply these principles in every project.
