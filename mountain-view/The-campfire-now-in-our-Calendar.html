The Campfire, Now in Our Calendar

10 April 2026

"This meeting could just as well have been an email." The irony is: people have always gathered to talk. So how did we get here?

Humans have always come together for good conversations. From the campfire to natural gathering spots, to meeting rooms — and now, virtually.

  • ·We solve things better together.
  • ·We think better together.
  • ·We create better things together.

The problem isn't meetings

Meetings engage — or at least they should. I don't think meetings are the problem. Bad meetings are the problem.

And I believe meetings need to be reclaimed. As the place where we think together.

The campfire, now in our calendar.

Take back control

Start by asking yourself: Do I actually need a meeting? If the answer is yes, then have a purpose, a goal, a structure — and the courage to lead a meeting that truly engages.

One thing I've noticed: my meetings improved dramatically when I turned agenda items into questions instead. People thought ahead. They showed up better prepared.

For example:
"What are your three key priorities for the coming quarter?"
Instead of simply: Priorities.

Meetings are not a calendar item — they're a shared thinking space. Next time, make sure to make the most of it.

What's one thing you can change to make your next meeting a little more engaging?
