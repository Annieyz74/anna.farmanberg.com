Give Yourself Permission to Pause

23 July 2026

How come it's often the small, everyday moments we remember the most?

I picked a few blueberries for my yoghurt at the summer house today, and suddenly I was back in childhood. I remembered how my brother and I used to pick blueberries when our mum was making blueberry pie. The berries were tiny, the bowl was huge, and it felt like it took forever to fill it when we were little. I smiled at the memory, because if we wanted blueberry pie, we had to pick the berries ourselves.

It's a lovely childhood memory but I'm not entirely sure that I remember the things my parents think, or maybe hope, that I remember from growing up.

Many people are on holiday now, travelling or finally doing the things they don't have the time or energy for during the rest of the year. Some plan every day of their vacation, with activities and social gatherings back‑to‑back, others prefer the hammock. Whatever kind of summer you enjoy, it made me think about how summer memories are created and what we actually remember years later.

It turns out that it's often the small, everyday things, or moments tied to feelings or our senses in some way.

So, give yourself permission to pause in whatever way suits you best. Create memories you'll be happy to carry with you for years to come and enjoy the summer, which is pretty short here in the Nordics.

As for me, I'll enjoy my yoghurt with freshly picked blueberries and I can almost smell my mum's blueberry pie, still the best one there is.
