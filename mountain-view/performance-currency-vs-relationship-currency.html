Performance Currency vs. Relationship Currency

19 March 2026

Many years ago, a manager told me to be less social and less helpful at work. "Focus on your goals," was the advice.

But here's the thing: I've never believed that relationships distract from performance. For me, they enable it.

The social glue that makes work actually work

Over the years I've learned that relationships are the foundation that makes work actually work. I reach my goals through relationships — not instead of performance, but because trust accelerates performance.

  • ·Trust makes people collaborate.
  • ·Collaboration makes things move.

And when you're not in the room, it's relationships that make others speak up for you — and open doors you didn't even know existed.

Two currencies. One career.

I recently listened to a TEDx talk by Carla Harris, and she put perfect words to something I've lived intuitively for years. This is my interpretation of her message, in a more Nordic, trust-based context:

Performance Currency gets you noticed.
Relationship Currency takes you further.

That has been true for me throughout my entire career. I naturally lean toward the relationship side — but I've learned to navigate both.

Which currency do you invest in?
