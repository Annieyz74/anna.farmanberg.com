Who Met You Then?

11 June 2026

During the short graduation weeks in June, we are reminded of how powerful it is to be met by someone when you take a step into the unknown.

When students run out through the school doors, family, relatives and friends are usually waiting for them, handing over flowers and greeting them with bright smiles and open arms. It's a small audience that sees you and confirms that you are on your way. This is a significant transition in life that you do not have to make alone.

It struck me the other day how less common that is in working life, because we also make many leaps there — many big shifts that shape us and influence our direction.

  • We leave school and step into adulthood
  • We get our first job
  • We take on our first leadership role
  • We change roles or industries
It is a natural part of working life, yet unlike graduation day, these transitions often happen quietly.

Still, the need to be seen remains, because we are all moving from one chapter to the next, and those moments become easier when someone is there to meet you.

We can all play that role for one another, by being the person who welcomes someone into their next step. And maybe we can choose, a little more often, to be that person for someone else. The one who reminds them that it is completely natural to feel both brave and uncertain at the same time.

In the teams I have valued the most, there have always been people who choose to meet others as they step out of their comfort zone, and it has made a real difference both for performance and for the desire to stay.

Who met you then, and who do you meet now?
