Some Places Stay With You — And So Do Some Brands

31 March 2026

What is it that makes certain places stay with you long after you've left?

Last weekend I visited Krägga Herrgård, beautifully situated by Lake Mälaren, and felt how clearly a place can communicate its identity — without saying a word. Earlier that week I had written about personal branding, and I realised that places work exactly the same way.

It lives in the details

It's in the atmosphere. In how you're welcomed. In the small details and experiences. There's something wonderful about a place that simply lets its feeling speak.

The calm. The nature. The water. The light. The surroundings.
Friday BBQ. The food. Afternoon tea.
The meetings. The people. The conversations.
The photography workshop. The sauna and a dip in the lake.

It's all of this that stays with you — a feeling of warmth and belonging.

Authentic from the beginning

A strong brand — whether it belongs to a person, a company or a place — always starts with authenticity.

Krägga, with roots going back to the 14th century, carries its own voice, personality and story. And you can tell they keep developing it, step by step, without losing what makes them unique.

People and places have this in common: their identity is felt long before it's ever articulated. The same goes for organisations.

It makes me wonder how many companies and organisations truly let their own story come through.

Everyone has a story. The question is only whether we dare to show it.
