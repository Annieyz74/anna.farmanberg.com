It's Always About People, Never the Platform

18 June 2026

What's LinkedIn to you?

To me, LinkedIn is a global forum, a global enabler with huge potential. A place where you can reach people you'd never meet otherwise.

I've seen posts lately where people say they're "struggling", "giving up", or that the "new algorithm" is hurting their visibility. I understand the frustration, and it made me think about how social platforms work. Since the algorithms change all the time, they always have. And that's one of the reasons I like LinkedIn, because it's less about the algorithm and more about finding your people — whoever they might be.

In my view, the algorithm helps me discover relevant content, peers, and people who share a similar mindset or challenge it. And it also brings me perspectives I wouldn't have found on my own.

Moreover, I believe that people are much more alike than we might think, regardless of geography. I've seen the same thing in Customer Success, where I'd say that industry, market and culture specifics are maybe 10–30%. The rest is human behaviour, curiosity, values, and how we relate to each other. After all, people are people.

No single post is fighting an algorithm. It's just entering a massive, living and dynamic global conversation.

If we zoom out for a moment: LinkedIn has around 1.3 billion members. Imagine, it's millions of posts and thousands of new connections every single minute. When you look at it like that, it becomes clear.

So go with your flow and talk about what matters to you.

Remember, let the algorithm rather work with you than against you.
