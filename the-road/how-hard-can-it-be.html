How Hard Can It Be? — What Riding a Motorcycle Taught Me About Self-Leadership

16 April 2026

How hard can it be? We say it all the time, knowing it rarely is. To learn something new — a skill you practice, a competence you build, an ability you grow into. It turns out things are rarely as easy as they look from a distance.

I was determined and enthusiastic; it was time to fulfil my teenage dream: learning how to ride a motorcycle. How hard can it be?

Speed is the easy part

I've always been pretty good with speed — the movement, the flow and the momentum. But then I faced what many new riders struggle with: the slow-speed maneuvering course. The ultimate test of precision and control. A totally different story.

Riding taught me something I didn't expect: it's easy to feel confident when you're moving fast. It's much harder when everything slows down and every small adjustment suddenly matters.

I spent hours practicing slow-speed technique, balance and counter-leaning. Thanks to my excellent instructors — and my own grit, resilience and determination — something finally clicked. I felt like a winner.

The giant puzzle

Learning to ride a motorcycle is like assembling a giant puzzle. Small steps, piece by piece, tiny adjustments and countless hours of practice. And then one day, the pieces fall into place. Integrated, automated and part of your system.

It took me two summers to fulfil my dream, and it was worth every hour, every failure, every lesson and every small win. I grew. And this experience taught me something important: after this, there's nothing I can't handle.

As my instructor kept telling me: "It's all in your head." He's the greatest pedagogue I've ever met — and he was right. It is all in your head.

Why some people keep going

Why am I bringing this up? Because we often underestimate the power of motivation and self-leadership in challenging situations — both in our professional and personal lives.

Why do some people keep going while others stop? I believe motivation and self-leadership are two of the most important keys. We all have them within us.

Think of a challenge you've faced — or are facing right now. What would following through look like?

Go for your dreams.
