Your Inner Engine — What Actually Keeps You Going

23 April 2026

Imagine spending hours adjusting the clutch one millimeter at a time just to go even slower on the slow-speed maneuver course. Counter-leaning. Balancing. Repeating. In every type of weather. Oh yes, pouring rain and storm included. And failing. Tremendously.

How do you motivate yourself to keep going?

I shared my motorcycle journey last week, and today I want to share what kept me going despite despair and failure — the lessons learnt, the progress, the growth, the small wins.

There's more to motivation than we think

True motivation comes from within. Not from pressure, deadlines or external rewards, as we usually think it does.

And the newest neuroscience backs this up. Neuroscience now shows that true motivation is created when the brain experiences autonomy, competence and connection — not when we're pushed from the outside.

Inner motivation happens when it's optimal, matched, integrated and natural. Think of it as your inner engine. When it's aligned, it steadily pushes you in the right direction.

That's what kept me going. And yes, visualization helped too — seeing myself succeed long before I actually did. The feeling of being able to go for a ride whenever I wanted, wherever I wanted. The feeling of total freedom.

The small wins that carry you forward

The small wins are what make you continue. Quick feedback and a receipt that there is constant progress. Small wins build confidence over time. It's the small steps that take you forward.

Progress is tricky to grasp because it's quiet, subtle and easy to overlook. Until one day — it is not.

Small wins are rarely dramatic. It can be sending the email you've postponed, understanding one new concept, or simply showing up again after a difficult day.

What's next

What I didn't know back then is that there's a whole model behind this. A model that explains why some motivation drains us and why some motivation fuels us.
