Train Before It Really Matters — What a Motorcycle Taught Me About Preparation

8 May 2026

How do you react when something unexpected happens and you have to make a split-second decision? Last year, I faced one of those moments while riding my motorbike. I'm a new rider, and I had spent hours and hours practicing the basics. When it happened, I didn't think. My body did what it had been trained to do — an evasive manoeuvre.

The kind of knowledge we actually remember

This made me think about deep learning — the kind of knowledge we actually remember when things get tough. Because sooner or later, things will get tough, and we fall back on what we've practiced.

We don't rise to the level of our ambitions. We fall back on the level of our preparation.

Split-second moments at work

Just like on the bike, we face split-second moments at work too. Moments where the conversation changes direction, the room tilts a little, or something lands on the table without warning:

  • ·A customer escalation in the middle of a meeting
  • ·A deal slipping
  • ·An unexpected question from leadership
  • ·A workshop derailing
  • ·A tough conversation

In those moments, we have to think and act fast — but still professionally. And we need to be able to fall back on our preparation, our dry runs, our scenarios and our practice.

So, here's my question: How does your team train — before it really matters?
