Sharing Is Caring (Or Maybe the Other Way Around)

25 June 2026

How good are you at asking for help when you want to get better at something?

In sports, it’s obvious: you improve faster when you get professional help and practice, even as a happy amateur. My putting (golf) has been way off lately, so today I took a lesson with a Pro. Time to train.

It’s Not Just About Sport

In working life, there are coaches and mentors, but often there are already people around us who are both knowledgeable and experienced. And most are happy to share what they know, if someone simply asks.

Over the years, I’ve become better at asking for help and feedback, because I’ve realised that doing things alone isn’t strength. Experience-sharing is one of the most valuable ways to grow. And as more companies move toward software and AI, complexity increases. I’d say we need more knowledge-sharing today than ever.

Three Things to Try

So why not identify 1–3 things you want to learn more about going forward?

  • ·Decide which people you want to ask
  • ·Invite them to a short knowledge-sharing session — a coffee, a lunch, or a quick 20–30 minute meeting
  • ·Keep it simple — it doesn’t have to be more complicated than that

My experience is that very few people say no. Most are simply happy to be asked, and it often sparks curiosity and learning on both sides.

Sharing is caring, or maybe it was the other way around. Either way, it’s true.

Happy sharing!
