The Mid-Year Check-In: A Conversation With Yourself

16 July 2026

When was the last time you did a check-in with yourself? I believe that the middle of the year is a natural checkpoint and summer gives us the space to actually use it. It's a great time to do a mid-year check-in and reflect on the past and coming months.

Attendee: Me, myself & I
Total time: 2-3 hours: split in two sessions, each 60-90 minutes
Optional: Micro‑block 15–20 min

Block 1: Reflection

This covers the "What did I learn / What worked / What was difficult / What should I let go of" part. This is the deeper part, it takes time to remember, connect dots and see patterns.

You'll need:

  • Quiet
  • A notebook or doc
  • No interruptions
  • A slower pace
  1. What actually happened? What really unfolded and happened (not planned). Projects, decisions, changes, challenges, wins.
  2. What worked well? Behaviours, routines, choices, collaborations, habits that created progress or momentum.
  3. What felt difficult or slow? Understand context, don't judge yourself. Energy dips, external factors, timing, workload, market conditions.
  4. What did I learn? Skills, insights, patterns, personal growth, professional development.
  5. What am I proud of? Most people skip this, but it's essential. Moments of courage, consistency, improvement, or resilience.
  6. What should I let go of? Expectations, projects, ideas, habits or goals that no longer serve you.

Block 2: Forward Thinking

This covers the "What can I improve / What can I do differently / What should I learn / What do I want to prioritise" part. This block is faster because: you already have the raw material from Block 1. Shift into direction-setting decisions come more naturally once reflection is done.

  1. What did I learn in the first half of the year? This anchors your direction. It helps you see patterns, strengths and shifts that matter going forward.
  2. What can I improve? In a practical and professional way. Think behaviours, routines, communication, focus, boundaries. NB! Avoid self‑criticism.
  3. What can I do differently? This is the "small adjustments" question. Tiny changes often create the biggest movement.
  4. What should I learn next? Skills, tools, topics, methods – especially those that support your goals for autumn.
  5. What do I want to prioritise? Choose 2–3 things, prioritization creates movement.
  6. What do I need to feel energized and steady? This is the sustainability question. Rest, structure, support, rhythm.

Optional: Micro-block (15–20 min)

This helps you avoid overloading the plan. A short "closing" block the next day to:

  • Refine your priorities
  • Choose your top 2–3 focus areas
  • Write a short summary
  • Decide what goes into August vs September

I like a small reset before the pace picks up again after summer. For me, one focus area for the coming months is strengthening my professional network after summer, something I want to work with more intentionally.

If you're doing your own mid‑year review, I'd love to hear what you're focusing on for the months ahead.
