Which Slope Are You On? — The Learning Curve in a Ski Resort

28 April 2026

Have you ever thought about the expression "In my book" — or, in Swedish, I min värld? I have, and I keep wondering why we use it.

We all know people have different perspectives, experiences, skills and competences. Yet we often say "In my book…" when we struggle to understand someone else's mindset or actions.

Imagine a ski resort

People step off the gondola or ski lift and choose a slope based on their skill level — from green to black. I look at people the same way.

In some situations, I'm enjoying the red or black slopes. Next time, I'm a complete beginner on the green one, doing everything I can to keep my dignity. Just like my first hours on a motorcycle, or my first lesson learning how to snowboard.

We're all on several slopes at once. Confident on one, learning on another, struggling on a third.

We're all learners — and the slope we're on today is rarely the one we'll be on tomorrow.

The learning curve, again and again

That's why I like the ski metaphor. It mirrors the learning curve — a process we move through again and again: curiosity, frustration, uncertainty, productivity and, eventually, plateau.

Look around you. Which slope are your colleagues, friends or family on? Do they look like they need guidance or support?

And — what's the color of the slope you're on right now?
