One Size Fits All — And Why It Never Does

24 March 2026

One size. Fits all. It's never true.

My favourite clothing store proves it every season. It's rarely flattering. And if we're honest, it probably fits around 30% of people — at best.

It's a "safe middle" concept that tries to be inclusive but ends up doing the opposite. For my Swedish friends: yes, "safe middle" is the closest I can get to mellanmjölk.

The problem with designing for everyone

A few people feel great. The rest are left feeling like something is wrong with them. And it struck me that we do the exact same thing with learning.

  • ·We expect people to fit into the same model.
  • ·The same template.
  • ·The same structure.

We do it in onboarding. In training. In processes designed to "work for everyone."

But people don't work like that

We learn differently. We need different things. We take in the world in different ways.

"One size fits all" is convenient for the one who creates it — not for the one who's supposed to use it.

How do you work? What's your "one size fits all" moment — the thing that simply doesn't work for you?
