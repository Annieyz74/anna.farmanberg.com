AI as a Thinking Partner

26 May 2026

What is AI to you? For some, it's technology. For others, it's uncertainty, potential, or prompting.

If we zoom out, innovation has always been part of the human story: Fire, tools, the wheel, wind, water, steam, and electricity. Each shift changed how we lived, worked and collaborated.

In my view, they all had one thing in common: They were human-centric, they amplified something, and most importantly — they required new behaviours.

Innovators and early adopters pushed the boundaries, and over time communities, cultures and societies adapted. It didn't happen overnight, and there were certainly challenges along the way. It took iteration, prototypes, refinement and above all: collaboration.

Where are we on the curve?

Now, AI is everywhere, and it feels messy. So, I started thinking about Gartner's hype cycle, because your perspective depends entirely on where you place yourself on that curve.

Societies are moving away from the Peak of Inflated Expectations. And AI isn't hype anymore — it demands ownership.

Knowledge workers tend to fall into three groups:

  1. Still in the hype — "AI should do everything for me."
  2. In the trough — "AI is overrated; it doesn't work."
  3. Early enlightenment — "AI works, if I work with it in a structured way."

It's group three that is now driving the real development forward.

Organizations? Leadership teams often shift between excitement and concern. Decisions can feel scattered because they're jumping on the curve rather than being on it.

AI professionals and early adopters are already climbing the Slope of Enlightenment, where real value is created.

So where is the collective? I'd say somewhere between the Peak of Inflated Expectations and the Trough of Disillusionment. It varies — and it always will.

AI as an amplifier

We're now in the AI shift, and I hear and see a lot about prompting and AI agents. What I'd like to emphasize is the value of using AI as a Thinking Partner and an amplifier.

No matter where you are on the curve — hype, trough or enlightenment — you can always start by asking AI a question.

As a partner that helps you think, reflect and move forward. You already have everything you need to begin. Just reach out to your preferred model in the same way you'd reach out to a colleague.

I believe that this is a very good start of any valuable partnership.

Remember, AI isn't one thing — it's a thousand perspectives.
