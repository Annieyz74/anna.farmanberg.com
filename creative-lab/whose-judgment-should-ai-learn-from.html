Whose Judgment Should AI Learn From?

16 June 2026

We keep hearing that human judgment is what sets us apart from AI, but what do we actually mean by judgment?

To me judgment is subjective, it's based on my values, knowledge and experiences. But in a company, judgment is more than personal. It's shaped by the brand, the culture, the values and the unwritten rules that guide how people read situations, weigh options and decide what feels right in the moment.

A shared way of knowing what's right and "how we do things here". And the interesting part is that this shared judgment is rarely written down in full, because it lives in people, in how they read a situation, weigh the options and decide.

AI is great at analyzing, summarizing and spotting patterns across huge amounts of information. But as you know, judgment is something completely different. It's knowing when to follow the data and when to trust your gut. When to make an exception or when something feels off, even if you can't explain why.

We talk about judgment as if it were objective and stable, when in reality it's deeply subjective.

And maybe that's the real challenge in all of this. Most of us quietly assume that our own judgment is the reasonable, balanced and fair one simply because it's familiar and has served us well. That's human — we trust what we know, and our own experience feels safe.

The question I keep coming back to: whose judgment should AI learn from?

Perhaps that's the question we should be asking.
