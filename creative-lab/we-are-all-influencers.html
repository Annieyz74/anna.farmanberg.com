We Are All Influencers

19 May 2026

Did you know that we make thousands of decisions every day? Big or small, conscious or unconscious — it adds up. Thankfully, our brain automates most of them for us. That's probably why we tend to stick to what's familiar.

But some decisions matter more. Those are the ones that need our attention, energy and intention. And this is where we all become influencers — not the Instagram or TikTok kind, but the everyday kind. The kind that shapes conversations, alignment and outcomes.

Think about a moment when you successfully influenced a decision — and a moment when you didn't. The contrast usually tells you everything.

I've always found communication fascinating, especially the psychology behind it. For me, it often comes down to setting the scene and creating context. Finding common ground and helping the other person step into the right frame of mind. So how do you do that without being pushy?

Behavioural science offers a great toolbox — when mixed with respect:

  • Priming — set the scene and create shared context, shaping the frame just enough to support the dialogue.
  • Nudging — gently guiding people in a helpful direction.
  • Timing — when is it a good time to influence and have meaningful dialogues?
  • Respect — because communication is always a two-way street.

It's all about small signals and the right timing. And suddenly, decisions become easier for everyone involved.

We influence each other more than we realise. And when we do it with intention and respect, dialogues become a lot more meaningful. Because better decisions can be made when people understand and commit together.

What signals do you pay attention to when influencing or communicating with others? And how do you make sure it becomes a dialogue, rather than a monologue?
