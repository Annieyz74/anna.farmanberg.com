Finding Your Sweet Spot

21 May 2026

Sweet spot A point, range or particular set of conditions that achieve the most desirable or effective outcome. That I'm invincible feeling when everything flows and goes our way. The spot we're all chasing, the moment when everything aligns. The positive state you are in when your mind and body work together and you just know what and how to do it.

In work life it's about the momentum, great collaboration, learning, progress, development, the wins and the successes. In sports it's about precision, timing and technique. But the sweet spot is more than precision — it's also about context.

I had a good opportunity to think a lot about the sweet spot, or rather the absence of it, during my last round of golf. A great start at the practice range, I had a lot of sweet spot moments and I felt awesome. "This will be a great round of golf," I said to myself. Then, I somehow lost that feeling completely. Sometimes you don't even see the water hazards, and other days it's water, woods and rough everywhere. Some days are simply way off.

This made me think:

  • Why are some rounds a great experience?
  • Why are others a four-hour struggle?
  • What stays constant and what doesn't?
  • What can we influence — and what can't we?

In my case my golf equipment is actually the only constant in my golf example. Everything else changes from time to time:

  • The Club/golf course
  • The course's condition and set up (Grass, green, fairway, flag positions etc.)
  • The weather
  • My mental & physical state
  • Different three players in the group of four

And it's not just golf. Anyone who plays a precision sport — tennis, padel, badminton — knows the feeling. One day every shot sits and the next day nothing does.

Effortless takes effort

The thing is that it takes a lot of energy, grit, determination and practice to get there. You need to expose yourself to different conditions, even the tough ones to learn and develop. And you also need to have the strategy and game plan ready.

Golf and work life have more in common than you might think. It's all in your head; mindset, perspective, precision and practice matter a lot. It's about strategy and tactics, to control the context as much as possible, managing people, expectations and mitigating risks.

Understanding that even when you are prepared, the world around you changes — people included.

In Customer Success, the mission is to help customers reach their objectives and succeed. (Customers own their goals but through dialogue, questions and insight, we can influence how those goals translate into actionable objectives where real impact happens.) But to do that, we also need to think about our own sweet spot and success. Because our ability to help others succeed is directly linked to how well we prepare, train, think and take care of ourselves.

So, the question I keep coming back to is:

How do we actually train and prepare to give ourselves the best possible chance of finding our sweet spot at work?
