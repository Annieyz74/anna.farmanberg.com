Make AI Relevant to Me — The Real Ask Behind the Hype

8 April 2026

81,000 people are probably right: "Make AI relevant to me."

Anthropic interviewed people across the world in December 2025 about AI. And I can completely relate to the main message: people aren't asking for more AI — they're asking for more relevance.

What people actually want from AI

The top three hopes are surprisingly human:

  • ·Professional excellence — 18.8%
  • ·Personal transformation — 13.7%
  • ·Life management — 13.5%

When people talk about AI, they're really talking about the essence of hyper-personalization: being understood, reducing noise, getting support at the right moment, having technology adapt to them — not the other way around.

The real gap

Technology offers personalization, but the real challenge lies in how organizations provide context. AI needs more than data. It needs organizational folklore.

Not the data in dashboards — but the data in people's heads. The knowledge that lives in post-it notes, side conversations, someone's memory, "the way we've always done it" — scattered across different systems, Excel sheets, PowerPoints, Word docs and team silos.

Folklore is the operating system behind the official processes. It's the difference between how things should work and how they really work.

Where Customer Success shifts

With folklore, AI becomes relevant. And this is exactly where the Customer Success role shifts — and where real value is created.

CS moves from doer → to orchestrator of context. Customer Success becomes the function that interprets the customer's reality, connects technology and people, sees the whole picture and creates relevance in every interaction.

Hyper-personalization is a shift in how we work:

  • ·From data → to understanding
  • ·From automation → to judgment
  • ·From generic → to personal
  • ·From noise → to relevance

AI transformation grows when technology, people, and organizational folklore work together.

Relevance isn't a technical problem — it's a knowledge problem. And the solution starts with understanding our own folklore.
